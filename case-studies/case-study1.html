Discovery

Gathering
insights.

We took an iterative approach to create the product, tackling the core functionality so that we had a sound basis on which to test.

The users we interviewed told us that the current time submission application is unhandy, has confusing UI, and complicated flows.

While essential to the business, there were inconsistent perspectives on how time should be tracked and submitted. Besides, time submission is a distraction from customer engagements.

After our discovery sessions, we were able to determine the following three high-level goals.

  • Automate repetitive processes with our available data.
  • Make it fast and easy to enter and submit the time for everyone, everywhere.
  • Give users more control of their time on customer engagements and the immediate demands of their role.

“With all of the information already available, employees wondered why certain processes were not automated and intuitive, allowing them to focus on the immediate demands of their role.”

Peter Persona

Ideate & create

Bringing
it all
to Life.

Our discovery sessions fed into ideation sessions where we collaborated to sketch out our initial ideas, followed by cycles of detailed design.

Full user flows and unique interactions are prototyped for sharing internally and testing with users. We prototyped a concept that focused on user personas and how the software contributed to their daily work.

Validation

User
testing.

To take the guesswork out of our design decisions, we validated them with real users in our office by bribing them with doughnuts and used a remote user research platform to validate with our partners in other regions.

Basing the design around user goals and iterating on user feedback allowed us to optimise the UI so that users can complete processes efficiently and without the repetitive hacks and workarounds they are used to carrying out.

Features

Seamless
experience.

Automate tasks.

The new time tracking application connects to data, which already exists in the organisation.

The app uses the power of Einstein to make the connection between the PSE project, team, and opportunities to preload relevant information into the time tracking application, saving the user the time of having to search and add assignments.

Einstein Voice.

Utilizing the latest advancements in Einstein Voice, employees can now create meetings on the go and have them directly connected to their PSE project.

Other guests on the invite will also receive the notification to add the meeting to their time card.

Seamless experience.

No more jumping back and forth between your calendar and the TimeCard app.

Connecting with the organisations inbox allows employees to connect their calendar directly to their time card. Intelligently scanning for keywords in invites, calendar events are linked to the appropriate projects.

location-based tracking.

Information within the organisation is used to identify when you’ve changed location from HQ to the client’s office.

Instead of calculating how much time travelled vs onsite, employees can turn on location-based time tracking to have the time automatically logged for the appropriate project.

Timely, notifications & intuitive actions.

When employees are not using the app, they are able to receive relevant and contextual information related to their time card.

Utilizing the latest in notification behavior, employees can swipe to dismiss, tap to open the notification, or hard press for a detail view allowing them to the appropriate action.

A voice-driven experience.

Leveraging advancements from the new partnership between the organisation and Apple to provide a way for users to quickly submit their time without even opening or looking at the app.

The TimeCard app provides the essential high-level information necessary to take action.
